    • Charles Williams

    Charles Walter Stansby Williams was a British poet, novelist, playwright, theologian, literary critic. He was born in Spetember 20, 2996 and died on May 15, 1945. He wrote 9 fiction books and stories, 18 plays, 7 volumes of poetry, 6 theological books, 10 works of literary criticism, 8 biographies, and 9 other works.

    In 1917 Williams married his first sweetheart, Florence Conway, following a long courtship during which he presented her with a sonnet sequence that would later become his first published book of poetry, The Silver Stair. Their son Michael was born in 1922. Williams was an unswerving and devoted member of the Church of England, reputedly with a tolerance of the scepticism of others and a firm belief in the necessity of a "doubting Thomas" in any apostolic body.
    Williams is buried in Holywell Cemetery in Oxford. His headstone bears the word "poet" followed by the words "Under the Mercy", a phrase often used by Williams himself.
