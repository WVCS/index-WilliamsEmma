    • Who were the Inklings?

    The Inklings were a group of writers at Oxford that met in the 1930s and 1940s. They regularly met at a pub named The Eagle and Child to discuss their works. While the writers wrote on a variety of topics and genres, they encouraged each other to write fantasy. The group consisted of:

    • Charles Williams, who is best known for writing Descent into Hell, War in Heaven, and The Place of the Lion, among other books and poems.
    • C. S. Lewis, who is known best for writing The Chronicles of Narnia, Screwtape Letters, and Mere Christianity, among other books.
    • J. R. R. Tolkien, who is best known for writing The Lord of the Rings series and The Hobbit, among other books.
    • Owen Barfield, a philosopher who is best known for writing The Silver Trumpet, History in English Words, and Night Operation, among other books and poems.
